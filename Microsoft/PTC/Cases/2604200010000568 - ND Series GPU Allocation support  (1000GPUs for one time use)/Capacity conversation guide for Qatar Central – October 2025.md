Capacity conversation guide for Qatar Central – October 2025
The following example conversation script has been developed to help field sellers discuss the capacity situation in Qatar 
Central with their customers. This document is Microsoft Confidential. You may customize it to address your customers 
specific resource asks. As a reminder, please DO NOT engage the press directly – leverage corporate communications to 
respond to press inquiries.
Please check aka.ms/azurecapacity for answers to common questions, the latest mitigation timelines and guidance to 
escalate urgent customer requests
Due to heightened customer demand in the Qatar Central datacenter region, we have implemented capacity preservation 
measures to ensure capacity is prioritized for existing customers that are already deployed in the region. These preservation 
measures are changes in our approval policies that result in restrictions for new customers and additional reviews of requests 
to increase quota from existing customers. These efforts are made to prioritize customers to continue growing in line with their 
pre-approved quota, to mitigate capacity exhaustion that could be caused by unexpected usage spikes, and to prevent new 
deployments from failing. Our first priority is always to protect the interests of existing customers in a region and allow them 
the ability to grow within their approved quota. Since many Azure environments need to expand/contract in an organic way to 
respond to demand, ensuring this ‘buffer’ for existing solutions takes precedence over new workloads.
This includes prioritising existing customer workloads to ensure customers have room to grow and scale. In addition, we do 
not expect the situation to impact disaster recovery planning, as we factor in headroom for these workloads as part of our 
planning process. 
We understand the <customer> team has requested an additional <n> compute core quota across multiple projects, but at 
this time we can’t automatically approve this increase and will review the request. We have instead recommended that for the 
best experience, new workloads should target the following regions:
• For organizations with Qatari government contracts, the Qatar Government entities are open to discuss deployments 
outside of Qatar region on a case-by-case basis. Ex: A lot of the Qatar government entities are considering to leverage 
Dynamics 365 services from other regions outside of Qatar. You need to get Qatari government approval for using 
Sweden Central if there are data residency restrictions. In case, there are very stringent requirements to have the 
application hosted in Qatar Central, please follow the required capacity escalation process (Capacity Escalation 
Process) for the case to be triaged.
• For organizations without specific Qatari data privacy requirements, please consider any other datacenter region 
around the globe as it aligns with latency, cost, product availability requirements.
I appreciate that you may have regulatory constraints that require this particular region – if that is the case, we will work to 
expedite and prioritize your request. Several contributing factors led to the decision to implement preventative restrictions in 
the Qatar Central datacenter region:
• We have seen a significant acceleration in customers relying on our cloud computing platform in this region, 
temporarily outpacing our supply forecast
• Delays in several datacenter construction projects in the region are impeding new capacity in the region
• Delay in a network upgrade project in the region limits our ability to deploy capacity in our existing facilities
With all of this in mind, we are bringing additional capacity into the region via:
• Working to expedite our expansion in the region, at existing facilities in Qatar
• We are continuing to closely monitor the situation and working to increase available capacity in the Qatar Central 
region, in line with customer demand signals.
