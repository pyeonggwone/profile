The best way to understand the usefulness of the Prosci ADKAR Model is to apply it to a  
personal situation. Using a situation you are close to will help you become familiar with the  
five elements that comprise the ADKAR Model. Begin by identifying a change you are having  
difficulty influencing another person (a family member, friend, neighbor or work associate)  
to make. Complete the worksheet to the best of your ability, rating each element on a scale  
of 1 (e.g., no awareness) to 5 (e.g., complete awareness). Be sure you select a change that the  
person isn’t adopting, despite your continued efforts to influence them.

Briefly describe a personal change in behavior you are trying to influence a friend, family member,  
neighbor or work associate to make:

> A senior colleague on my team has a Microsoft 365 Copilot license but does not use it in daily work. I am trying to influence him to regularly use Copilot for routine tasks — such as drafting emails, summarizing Teams meeting notes, and writing weekly status updates — instead of doing everything manually.

List the reasons you believe this change is necessary:

> 1. **Team credibility**: Our team advises customers on AI adoption. A consultant who does not personally use the tool they recommend loses credibility in customer conversations.
> 2. **Time savings**: Email drafting, meeting summaries, and status reports consume 1–2 hours daily. Copilot can reduce this significantly.
> 3. **Team initiative**: The team is launching a structured Copilot adoption drive. Senior engineers need to model the behavior for junior members.
> 4. **Skill development**: Hands-on Copilot fluency prepares engineers for deeper AI advisory work with customers.
> 5. **License utilization**: The license is already paid for — not using it is a missed opportunity.

List the factors or consequences (good and bad) for this person that  
create a desire to change:

> **Factors that create desire:**
> - Saving time on repetitive writing tasks
> - Peer pressure as teammates begin sharing Copilot wins publicly
> - Responsibility to model behavior for junior engineers
> - Staying relevant as AI fluency becomes a baseline expectation
>
> **Factors that resist desire:**
> - Current manual workflow feels familiar and reliable
> - Learning a new tool requires upfront time investment during an already overloaded schedule
> - No formal requirement or consequence for not using Copilot
> - Skepticism about output quality ("I write better myself")
