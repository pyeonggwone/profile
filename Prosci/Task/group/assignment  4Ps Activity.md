# 4 P's Model Canvas

## Project: MCEM Agentic Transformation

### Adoption Challenge

**What percentage of project outcomes depends on people doing their job differently?**

**Approximately 80%.**

The technical work of de-duplicating, restructuring, tagging, and publishing MCEM content is important, but the business outcome depends heavily on people changing how they create, approve, govern, and use MCEM guidance. If Core teams, SMEs, Sales Enablement Leads, Business Strategy & Operations, field leaders, and MCEM Council members continue to maintain duplicated or role-specific content outside the governed model, the Copilot and agent experiences will not be trusted or adopted.

---

## Project

**What is the project?**

The project is the **MCEM Agentic Transformation** for Microsoft Customer Engagement Model content. It will establish a trusted, role-relevant single source of truth by de-duplicating and restructuring MCEM content into a governed **unique facts** model, including canonical facts, exceptions, role-specific guidance, taxonomy, metadata, and governance processes.

The transformed content layer will power Copilot and agent experiences that help sellers, technical specialists, customer success teams, services teams, partner roles, and governance stakeholders find the right MCEM guidance at the right time.

## Purpose

**Why are we changing?**

MCEM content is currently fragmented and duplicated across segments, roles, and stakeholder groups. This creates inconsistent guidance, inefficient content maintenance, role confusion, and reduced trust in future Copilot or agent experiences. A field seller or partner role may receive different answers depending on the content source, the owner, or the segment-specific version they access.

The change is needed to:

- Create one governed source of truth for MCEM facts, exceptions, and guidance.
- Improve field readiness and consistency across Enterprise, SME&C, SMB, partner, customer success, services, and support roles.
- Reduce duplicated content maintenance across Core, SMEs, SEL, and BSO.
- Make MCEM content reliable enough to support Copilot and agent experiences.
- Strengthen governance alignment through the MCEM Council and segment leaders.
- Improve seller and partner outcomes by making guidance easier to find, trust, and apply.

**Risk of not changing:** MCEM guidance remains fragmented, duplicated, and inconsistently interpreted. Copilot and agent experiences may surface outdated or conflicting answers, which would reduce field trust and limit adoption.

## Particulars

**What are we changing?**

The project changes the structure, ownership, governance, and usage of MCEM content.

| Area | Current State | Future State |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| Content structure | Duplicated guidance across roles, decks, pages, and segment-specific assets | Unique facts model with canon, exceptions, and role-specific guidance |
| Taxonomy and metadata | Inconsistent or incomplete tagging by role, segment, topic, and usage context | Standard metadata model aligned to MCEM roles, segments, and governance needs |
| Content ownership | Distributed ownership with unclear decision rights for conflicts or updates | Defined owners, reviewers, and governance forums for content decisions |
| Content maintenance | Updates happen in multiple places and may drift over time | Updates flow through a governed model that preserves consistency |
| Field consumption | Users search across fragmented sources and rely on personal knowledge | Users access role-relevant guidance through trusted content and Copilot/agent experiences |
| Governance | Council and stakeholder alignment exists, but operational rules may vary | Governance aligned across MCEM Council, Core, SEL, BSO, SMEs, and field stakeholders |

**What is not changing:**

- MCEM remains the guiding model for customer engagement.
- Field roles such as ATU, STU, CSU, CSA, PSS, ISD, Services, and partner roles remain accountable for their customer and partner outcomes.
- Human subject matter experts continue to validate content and resolve exceptions.
- Copilot and agents support decision-making and content access; they do not replace business ownership or governance accountability.

## People

**Who will be changing?**

The change impacts both the governance layer and the field execution layer.

| Group | Current Behavior | Required New Behavior | Key ADKAR Consideration |
|-------|------------------|-----------------------|--------------------------|
| MCEM Council and executive leaders | Align priorities and provide strategic direction | Actively sponsor the unique facts model, resolve cross-segment conflicts, and reinforce governance decisions | Awareness and Desire are likely strong; Reinforcement must be sustained |
| Core content and program teams | Create and maintain central guidance | Convert content into canonical facts, exceptions, reusable guidance, and governed metadata | Knowledge and Ability will be critical because the work method changes significantly |
| Segment SMEs | Provide role and segment expertise, often through local content | Validate facts, define legitimate exceptions, and avoid rebuilding duplicated guidance | Desire may vary if local autonomy feels reduced |
| Sales Enablement Leads | Package and distribute readiness content | Teach the field how to use the new content model and agent experiences | Ability and Reinforcement are key because they influence adoption at scale |
| BSO and operations stakeholders | Support planning, reporting, and operational alignment | Use common taxonomy and governance rules for reporting and operational execution | Knowledge gap around metadata and governance rules |
| Field roles: ATU, STU, CSU, CSA, PSS, ISD, Services | Search and apply guidance from multiple content sources | Use trusted role-relevant guidance and provide feedback when content is unclear or incorrect | Desire depends on trust, speed, and relevance of the new experience |
| Copilot/agent product or delivery teams | Build experiences from available content | Design experiences around governed content, traceability, and feedback loops | Ability depends on clear metadata and content quality |

**Highest impact groups:** Core content teams, SMEs, SEL, BSO, and field-facing roles. These groups must change daily work patterns for the project to deliver value.

**Primary people-side success measure:** impacted stakeholders consistently create, govern, and consume MCEM content through the unique facts model instead of maintaining or relying on duplicated unmanaged sources.
