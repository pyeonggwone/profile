# Prosci Risk Assessment

## Project: MCEM Agentic Transformation

This assessment evaluates the people-side risk for establishing a governed MCEM unique facts model that will power trusted Copilot and agent experiences.

Scale used: **1 = lower risk / smaller change**, **5 = higher risk / larger change**.

---

---

## Overall Risk Position

| Dimension | Score | Risk Level |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Change Characteristics | 43 / 60 | High complexity |
| Organizational Attributes | 35 / 60 | Moderate-to-high resistance risk |

**Overall risk rating: High.**

The project should be treated as a high people-side risk change because it is broad, cross-functional, and behavior-dependent. Even if the content model is technically sound, adoption will fail if stakeholders do not trust the governed model, stop duplicating content, and consistently use the new source of truth.

---

## Key People-Side Risks and Mitigation Actions

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Segment teams continue maintaining local versions of MCEM guidance | High | High | Define governance decision rights, exception rules, and reinforcement from MCEM Council sponsors. |
| SMEs do not have enough time to validate canonical facts and exceptions | High | High | Secure sponsor-backed time commitments and prioritize high-value content domains first. |
| Field users do not trust Copilot or agent answers | Medium | High | Provide traceability to governed content, publish content quality standards, and create a fast feedback loop. |
| Metadata and taxonomy are applied inconsistently | Medium | High | Create a clear tagging standard, examples, quality checks, and review workflow. |
| Sponsors are supportive but not visibly active | Medium | High | Build a sponsorship roadmap with visible communications, decision forums, and reinforcement actions. |
| Change fatigue reduces engagement | Medium | Medium | Connect the change to field pain points, reduce duplicate work, and communicate quick wins. |
| Managers and enablement leads are unclear on their role | Medium | Medium | Provide manager and SEL coaching guides with specific talking points and adoption expectations. |

---

## Recommended Change Management Focus

1. Build and maintain a strong sponsorship coalition across MCEM Council, Core, segment leaders, SEL, and BSO.
2. Segment impacted groups by role and define specific behavior changes for each group.
3. Use ADKAR diagnostics to identify whether each group is blocked by Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, or Reinforcement.
4. Prioritize communications around why trusted content is essential for reliable Copilot and agent experiences.
5. Reinforce the new model by retiring duplicated sources, recognizing early adopters, and measuring usage and content quality.
