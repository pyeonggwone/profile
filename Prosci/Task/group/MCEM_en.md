# MCEM — Microsoft Customer Engagement Model

## What is MCEM?

MCEM (Microsoft Customer Engagement Model) is Microsoft's internal framework that defines how the field organization engages with customers across the full lifecycle — from awareness and sales through deployment, adoption, and ongoing success. It standardizes roles, motions, content, and governance so that sellers, technical specialists, and customer success teams operate in a coordinated way.

MCEM is governed by the **MCEM Council**, which aligns strategy and execution across all major segments and functions.

---

## Segments

| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Enterprise** | Large enterprise accounts; deeply relationship-driven, complex multi-workload deals |
| **SMe&C** (SMC) | Small, Medium & Corporate; mid-market accounts with scaled commercial motions |
| **SMB** | Small and Medium Business; high-volume, partner-led, mostly digital motion |

---

## Key Roles in the Field

| Role | Full Name | Responsibility |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| **ATU** | Account Technology Unit | End-to-end account ownership; business relationship and deal orchestration |
| **STU** | Solution Technology Unit | Technical specialist sellers aligned to specific solution areas (e.g., Azure, Security, Modern Work) |
| **CSU** | Customer Success Unit | Post-sales technical enablement; drives adoption and usage after contract signing |
| **CSA** | Customer Success Account Manager | Manages the success plan and ongoing relationship for enterprise customers |
| **PSS** | Premier Support Specialist | Delivers proactive and reactive support services under Premier/Unified contracts |
| **ISD** | Industry Solutions Delivery | Delivers professional services and solution implementations |
| **Services** | Microsoft Consulting Services | Advisory, implementation, and managed services for strategic engagements |

---

## Cross-Cutting Functions

| Function | Role in MCEM |
|----------|--------------|
| **RTIs** | Ready-to-Inspire content assets; curated sales plays, pitch decks, and enablement materials used by field roles |
| **BSO** | Business Strategy & Operations; supports planning, reporting, and operational execution across segments |
| **SEL** | Sales Enablement Leads; responsible for readiness, training, and content deployment to the field |
| **Core** | Central content and program teams that define canonical guidance, governance, and policy |
| **MCEM Council** | Governing body that aligns segment leaders, Core, and field functions on strategy, priorities, and execution standards |

---

## Why MCEM Matters for the Agentic Transformation Project

The "MCEM Agentic Transformation" project targets the content layer that powers the MCEM model. Currently, MSEM (Microsoft Sales Enablement Model) content is fragmented and duplicated across roles and segments. The project aims to:

1. **De-duplicate and canonize** content into a "unique facts" model (canonical facts, exceptions, role-specific guidance)
2. **Restructure taxonomy and metadata** so content is tagged by role (ATU, STU, CSU, etc.) and segment
3. **Enable Copilot/agent experiences** that can reliably surface the right content to the right role at the right moment
4. **Align governance** across Core, SEL, BSO, and the MCEM Council to sustain content quality over time
