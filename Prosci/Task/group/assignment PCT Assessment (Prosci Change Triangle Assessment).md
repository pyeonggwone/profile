# Prosci PCT Assessment

## Project: MCEM Agentic Transformation

Scale used: **1 = inadequate**, **2 = adequate**, **3 = exceptional**.

---

## Sponsorship / Leadership

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| The change has a primary sponsor | 3 | Primary sponsors are identified as Alfredo Ramirez and Sarah Crowe. |
| Sponsor has authority over people, systems, and processes | 2 | Sponsors appear to have strong influence, but authority must be exercised through a distributed council and segment leadership model. |
| Sponsor can build coalition sponsorship and manage resistance | 2 | A coalition is possible, but it will require active engagement across MCEM Council, Core, segment leaders, SEL, BSO, and SMEs. |
| Sponsor will participate visibly and actively throughout the project | 2 | Sponsorship is defined, but visible actions and cadence should be formalized. |
| Sponsor will make decisions on scope, schedule, and resources | 2 | Sponsors can escalate and resolve decisions, but clear decision rights are needed for content conflicts and prioritization. |
| Sponsor can build awareness with employees about why the change is happening | 2 | The business reason is compelling, but role-specific messaging must be developed. |
| Organization has a clearly defined strategy and vision | 3 | The project aligns strongly to Microsoft's AI and field productivity direction. |
| Change is aligned with organization strategy and vision | 3 | Trusted content is essential for reliable Copilot and agent experiences. |
| Priorities have been communicated and set | 2 | The direction is clear, but competing initiatives and segment priorities need active management. |
| Sponsor will reinforce the change and celebrate success | 2 | Reinforcement mechanisms should be planned, including recognition of content owners and adopters. |

**Sponsorship / Leadership Score: 23 / 30**

Interpretation: **Alert / possible risk.** Sponsorship exists and the strategic case is strong, but the project needs a more explicit sponsorship roadmap and coalition plan.

---

## Project Management

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| The change is clearly defined, including future state and impacted groups | 3 | The project summary clearly defines the unique facts model and impacted roles. |
| Project scope is clearly defined | 2 | Scope is broad and clear directionally, but content domains and release boundaries should be prioritized. |
| Objectives that define success are specific | 2 | Objectives are meaningful, but measurable adoption, content quality, and usage metrics should be finalized. |
| Milestones and schedule have been identified | 2 | Hybrid delivery is identified; milestones should be made visible to all stakeholder groups. |
| Project manager assigned to manage tasks and resources | 2 | Project leadership is implied, but named PM ownership should be documented. |
| Work breakdown structure and deliverables identified | 2 | Major deliverables are known: content cleanup, taxonomy, metadata, governance, and agent readiness. Detailed WBS should be maintained. |
| Team resources identified and acquired | 2 | Stakeholder groups are identified, but SME and content owner capacity needs confirmation. |
| Periodic meetings scheduled to track progress and issues | 2 | Governance meetings are likely needed; cadence should include decision-making and issue escalation. |
| Primary sponsor readily available for issues affecting dates, scope, or resources | 2 | Sponsor availability should be built into escalation paths. |
| Project plan integrated with change management plan | 1 | Change management is not yet clearly integrated into the project plan. |

**Project Management Score: 20 / 30**

Interpretation: **Alert / possible risk.** The project has a strong business concept, but it needs stronger operational planning, metrics, resource confirmation, and integration with the change plan.

---

## Change Management

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Structured change management approach is being applied | 2 | Prosci tools are now being applied, but the approach should be formalized for execution. |
| Assessment of change and organizational impact completed | 2 | Initial impact is understood across stakeholder groups; more detailed role-based impact analysis is needed. |
| Organizational readiness assessment completed | 2 | Initial risk assessment is available; readiness by segment and role should be validated. |
| Anticipated resistance areas identified with tactics | 2 | Key risks are known, including local content ownership, SME capacity, trust, and change fatigue. Tactics need owners. |
| Change management strategy created, including sponsorship and CM team model | 1 | A full strategy and team model still need to be documented. |
| Change management team members identified and trained | 1 | Change team roles are not yet clearly identified. |
| Sponsorship coalition strength assessed | 1 | The coalition is known conceptually but has not been formally assessed. |
| Change management plans created for communications, sponsorship, coaching, training, and resistance | 1 | These plans need to be created and integrated with delivery milestones. |
| Feedback processes established to determine adoption | 2 | Feedback loops should be part of the agent/content model, but measures and channels need definition. |
| Resistance managed and successes celebrated | 1 | Reinforcement and recognition plans have not yet been defined. |

**Change Management Score: 15 / 30**

Interpretation: **High risk / immediate action needed.** The people-side work is the largest gap and should be addressed before broad rollout.

---

## PCT Summary

| Element | Score | Interpretation |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| Sponsorship / Leadership | 23 / 30 | Alert / possible risk |
| Project Management | 20 / 30 | Alert / possible risk |
| Change Management | 15 / 30 | High risk / immediate action needed |

**Overall PCT conclusion:** The project has a strong strategic rationale and visible sponsors, but it is not yet equally strong across all three sides of the Prosci Change Triangle. The most urgent improvement area is Change Management, followed by tighter integration between project delivery and people-side adoption activities.

## Recommended Action Steps

| Element | Action Step | Owner |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Sponsorship / Leadership | Create a sponsorship roadmap with specific visible actions, decision forums, communications, and reinforcement moments. | Primary sponsors with MCEM Council support |
| Sponsorship / Leadership | Build a sponsor coalition across MCEM Council members, Core, segment leaders, SEL, BSO, and key SMEs. | Primary sponsors |
| Project Management | Define release milestones, content domain priorities, success metrics, and governance decision rights. | Project manager and Core team |
| Project Management | Confirm SME and content owner capacity for validation, metadata tagging, and exception decisions. | Project manager and segment leaders |
| Change Management | Complete role-based impact assessments for content owners, SMEs, SEL, BSO, and field users. | Change management lead |
| Change Management | Build ADKAR-based communications, coaching, training, resistance, and reinforcement plans. | Change management lead with SEL |
