## Adoption Challenge

**What percentage of project outcomes depends on people doing their job differently?**

**100%**
The technology (Microsoft 365 Copilot) is already available and licensed. The entire outcome depends on whether team members change their daily work habits and use Copilot consistently for routine tasks.

---

## Project
*What is the project?*

**Title**: Structured Microsoft 365 Copilot Adoption

**Description**: An internal team (~30 people) is moving from occasional, self-directed use of Microsoft 365 Copilot to a structured, team-wide adoption where Copilot becomes the default tool for common daily work tasks.

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Initiative type | Internal behavioral change — no new technology deployment |
| Scope | One team, ~30 people |
| Timeline | 3 months |
| Sponsor | Department Manager |
| Delivery Leads | Team Leads |

**Tools in scope:**
- Microsoft 365 Copilot (already licensed and deployed)
- Microsoft Teams
- SharePoint (shared prompt library)

---

## Purpose
*Why are we changing?*

**Business Drivers:**
- Productivity pressure: team is overloaded and Copilot reduces 20–40 min/day of low-value writing and summarizing tasks
- Wasted license investment: licenses are deployed but usage is below 20%; investment not realized
- Inconsistent output quality: meeting notes, status reports, and emails vary widely across team members
- Credibility gap: a team advising on AI adoption must visibly practice AI use itself
- Future readiness: teams that embed AI habits now build durable productivity advantages

**Risk of Not Changing:**
Team continues at manual capacity; license spend is sunk with no return; employees fall progressively behind peers who are building AI fluency.

**Desired Outcomes:**

| Dimension | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|
| Individual | Each team member saves 20–40 min/day on routine writing and summarizing tasks |
| Team | Consistent, higher-quality meeting documentation, status reports, and internal communications |
| Organizational | Realized return on Copilot license investment; team positioned as AI-capable for future initiatives |
| Career | Team members develop AI fluency that is increasingly expected across all roles |

---

## Particulars
*What are we changing?*

**Before → After:**

| Task | Before | After |
|------|--------|-------|
| Email drafting | Written from scratch; often delayed | Copilot drafts from bullet points; person reviews and adjusts before sending |
| Meeting recap (Teams) | Rarely written; notes informal and incomplete | Copilot in Teams auto-generates recap and action items; team member reviews and distributes |
| Weekly status update | Manual write-up each week; repetitive effort | Copilot summarizes from emails and notes; person edits and confirms |
| Internal document prep | Blank-page writing; inconsistent format | Copilot generates first draft with structure; person completes and tailors |
| Product/feature research | Browser search; fragmented reading | Copilot summarizes key points from documents and release notes |

**What is NOT changing:**
- Individuals remain fully accountable for all content they send or submit
- No AI output goes to external stakeholders without human review
- Personal work style differences are respected — minimum expectation is 3 Copilot-assisted tasks per week

**Minimum Expectation:** Every team member uses Copilot as their default for at least 3 recurring work tasks within 3 months.

**Shared Assets to Build:**
- Prompt library (top 5 task types) — shared on SharePoint
- Validation norm: Copilot drafts, human reviews and approves

## People
*Who will be changing?*

**Total Impacted:** ~35 people | **Highest Impact Group:** Team Members (~30)

**Key Influencers:** Team Leads (closest peer role models), 2–3 identified early adopters

---

### Team Members (~30)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Current behavior | Write emails, recap meetings, and draft reports manually as default |
| Required new behavior | Use Copilot first for routine writing tasks; review output and send |
| WIIFM | Reduce daily cognitive load; save 20–40 min/day; stop rewriting the same report every week |
| ADKAR starting point | Awareness exists; **Desire is the gap** — most have tried Copilot once but not committed to changing their routine |
| Key resistance risk | Too busy with current work; first experience was not useful |

---

### Team Leads (~4)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Current behavior | Work alongside team without differentiating on tool use |
| Required new behavior | Use Copilot openly in team settings; share prompts that worked; ask what others tried |
| WIIFM | Build credibility as AI-capable leads; accelerate team performance without extra effort |
| ADKAR starting point | Generally positive; **Ability may vary** — some leads more comfortable with AI tools than others |
| Key resistance risk | Uncomfortable demonstrating something they have not fully mastered yet |

---

### Department Manager (1)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Current behavior | Verbally supportive of Copilot adoption; no formal communication or governance action taken |
| Required new behavior | Make adoption an explicit team goal; mention it in 1:1s; recognize early adopters; model personal use |
| WIIFM | Demonstrate AI-first leadership; improve team output quality and bandwidth |
| ADKAR starting point | High Awareness and Desire; **Knowledge and Ability gap** in active sponsorship behaviors |
| Key resistance risk | Limited time; may deprioritize sponsorship actions under competing management demands |
