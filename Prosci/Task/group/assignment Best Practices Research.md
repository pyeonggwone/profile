# Best Practices Research

## Project: MCEM Agentic Transformation

This research summary identifies change management best practices that should be applied to the MCEM Agentic Transformation project. The project will restructure MCEM content into a governed unique facts model and use that trusted content to power Copilot and agent experiences.

---

## Best Practice 1: Use Active and Visible Sponsorship

**Why it matters:** The project crosses multiple segments, roles, and governance layers. Without visible sponsorship, stakeholders may agree with the idea but continue maintaining local content sources because that feels faster or safer.

**Application to this project:**

- Alfredo Ramirez and Sarah Crowe should communicate why trusted MCEM content is required for reliable agentic experiences.
- Sponsors should chair or visibly support decision forums where content conflicts, exceptions, and priorities are resolved.
- MCEM Council members should act as local sponsors for their segments and functions.
- Sponsors should reinforce that the governed unique facts model is the expected source of truth.

**Expected benefit:** Stakeholders understand that this is not only a content cleanup exercise. It is a strategic operating model change for how MCEM guidance is created, governed, and consumed.

---

## Best Practice 2: Build a Sponsorship Coalition

**Why it matters:** A single sponsor cannot drive adoption across Enterprise, SME&C, SMB, partner, services, support, Core, SEL, and BSO audiences. Each group needs a credible leader who can translate the change locally.

**Application to this project:**

- Create a sponsor coalition map across MCEM Council members, segment leaders, Core, SEL, BSO, and SME communities.
- Define the role of each coalition member: communicate, make decisions, remove barriers, allocate SME time, and reinforce behaviors.
- Use coalition meetings to identify resistance, competing priorities, and unresolved content ownership issues.

**Expected benefit:** The project gains distributed leadership and can address local resistance before it blocks adoption.

---

## Best Practice 3: Segment Audiences by Impact and Required Behavior

**Why it matters:** Different groups are not experiencing the same change. A field seller using a Copilot answer has a different transition than a Core content owner restructuring canonical facts.

**Application to this project:**

| Audience | Primary Change | Change Management Focus |
|----------|----------------|-------------------------|
| Core content teams | Convert content into canonical facts, exceptions, and role guidance | Knowledge, Ability, quality standards |
| SMEs | Validate facts and exceptions instead of maintaining separate versions | Desire, time commitment, decision rights |
| SEL | Enable field readiness and teach new usage patterns | Knowledge, Ability, coaching |
| BSO | Align operations and reporting to common taxonomy | Knowledge, process alignment |
| Field roles | Use trusted role-relevant guidance and provide feedback | Awareness, Desire, trust, Reinforcement |
| MCEM Council | Govern priorities and resolve cross-segment decisions | Sponsorship, Reinforcement |

**Expected benefit:** Communications and training become relevant to each group instead of generic.

---

## Best Practice 4: Apply ADKAR to Diagnose Barriers

**Why it matters:** Resistance may not have one cause. Some stakeholders may not know why the change matters, while others may understand the vision but lack time, skills, or confidence in the new model.

**Application to this project:**

- **Awareness:** Explain the business problem: duplicated content creates inconsistent guidance and weakens agent trust.
- **Desire:** Connect the change to reduced rework, more trusted field answers, and better seller and partner outcomes.
- **Knowledge:** Train content owners and SMEs on the unique facts model, metadata standards, and exception handling.
- **Ability:** Provide working sessions, templates, examples, and quality reviews for content conversion.
- **Reinforcement:** Retire duplicated sources, recognize early adopters, measure content quality, and use leadership follow-up.

**Expected benefit:** The team can identify whether a group needs communication, motivation, training, practice, or reinforcement instead of applying one generic intervention.

---

## Best Practice 5: Integrate Change Management With the Project Plan

**Why it matters:** The PCT assessment shows that project management and change management must be integrated. If change activities happen after technical content work is complete, adoption risks will surface too late.

**Application to this project:**

- Add change management milestones to the project plan.
- Tie communications and training to content domain releases.
- Include stakeholder impact review in each major milestone.
- Make adoption readiness a release criterion, not a post-launch activity.
- Track risks such as SME capacity, local content duplication, trust in agent answers, and taxonomy quality.

**Expected benefit:** The project can deliver content capability and adoption readiness together.

---

## Best Practice 6: Create Feedback and Reinforcement Loops

**Why it matters:** Agentic experiences depend on trust. Field users must have a way to report unclear, outdated, or conflicting guidance, and content owners must see that feedback quickly.

**Application to this project:**

- Provide an easy feedback channel inside or alongside the Copilot/agent experience.
- Track feedback by role, segment, content domain, and severity.
- Define service levels for content correction and escalation.
- Use adoption and quality metrics in governance meetings.
- Celebrate examples where feedback improved field guidance or removed duplicate content.

**Expected benefit:** Users see that the system improves over time, which increases trust and sustained adoption.

---

## Recommended Best Practices for This Project

The top three best practices to prioritize are:

1. **Active and visible sponsorship** because the project requires cross-segment authority and sustained reinforcement.
2. **Audience segmentation and ADKAR diagnostics** because impacted groups have different behaviors, barriers, and success conditions.
3. **Integrated project and change management planning** because adoption risk must be managed during content transformation, not after rollout.

## Conclusion

The MCEM Agentic Transformation is a high-value change because it enables trusted, role-relevant guidance for Copilot and agent experiences. It is also a high people-side risk change because success depends on content owners, SMEs, enablement leads, governance stakeholders, and field roles changing how they work. Applying Prosci best practices will increase the likelihood that the project delivers more than a clean content model: it will help MCEM stakeholders adopt a sustainable way of creating, governing, and using trusted guidance.
