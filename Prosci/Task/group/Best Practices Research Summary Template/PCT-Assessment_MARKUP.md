"The project has a strong strategic rationale and visible sponsors, but it is not yet equally strong across all three sides of the Prosci Change Triangle. Recommended replacement note
The project has strong strategic rationale and high visibility from senior leaders, but the Prosci Change Triangle is structurally weakest on the Leadership/Sponsorship side, not on Change Management. There are multiple, unaligned senior sponsors (Steven Ross, Alfredo Ramirez, Sarah Crowe), and the answer to "what is MCEM, who owns its content lifecycle, and who lands MCEM changes in the field" is actively contested between my manager, skip, and skip's manager. Council members do not yet hold accountable ownership of content governance, and even less of landing. RTLs, SEL, and BSO further blur landing R&R relative to Council members. Until a single primary sponsor is named and a sponsor coalition is aligned on scope, vision, and R&R, every Change Management investment will under-deliver. The single highest-leverage next step is a sponsor alignment workshop that ends with a written, signed-off MCEM operating model and landing R&R map. Change Management workstreams (CM strategy, coalition assessment, integrated plans, feedback loop) should be designed against that aligned model, not in front of it.
