[Inputs into the change management process]
The change management process uses the project summary, 4Ps Activity, Prosci Change Triangle Assessment, Risk Assessment, and Best Practices Research as inputs. Key inputs include duplicated and fragmented MCEM content across MCAPS, the need for trusted Copilot/Agent experiences, and the need to align MCEM Council and segment stakeholders around a governed source of truth.

[Organizational benefits]
The organization will establish a governed unique facts model for MCEM content so field and governance stakeholders use the same source of truth. This will reduce content maintenance effort and duplicated work, improve the reliability and consistency of Copilot/Agent responses, and support more consistent seller, partner, and customer success outcomes.

[Project objectives]
The project will restructure MCEM content into canon, approved exceptions, and role-specific guidance while aligning taxonomy, metadata, ownership, review, and feedback processes. The end objective is to enable Copilot/Agent experiences to provide trusted MCEM guidance in the right role and business context.

[Adoption and usage objectives]
Core teams, SMEs, SEL, BSO, field roles, and the MCEM Council will use the governed unique facts model and agentic experiences instead of duplicated or local content sources. Content creators will follow the standard taxonomy and governance process, while field users will use trusted guidance and participate in the feedback loop.

[Units of measure]
Measures include duplicate content reduction, number of canonical facts converted, number of approved exceptions processed, metadata/tagging quality score, SME validation completion rate, Copilot/Agent usage, feedback resolution SLA, field trust and satisfaction, and progress in retiring local content sources.

[Benefits and objectives prioritized]
Priorities are: 1) establish a trusted source of truth, 2) clean up high-value content domains first, 3) define sponsor-backed governance decision rights, 4) build field trust and adoption, and 5) operate a sustainable feedback and reinforcement loop.

[Benefit and objective owners]
The primary sponsors are Alfredo Ramirez and Sarah Crowe. The MCEM Council owns governance and cross-segment decisions. Core teams and SMEs own content quality and validation. SEL and BSO support readiness, operational alignment, and field adoption.

[People dependency evaluation]
Approximately 80% of the project outcomes depend on people doing their work differently. Technical content cleanup is necessary, but the expected benefits will not be realized unless Core teams, SMEs, SEL, BSO, field roles, and the MCEM Council use the new governance model and Copilot/Agent experiences as the actual source of truth.

[Definition of success]
Success means MCEM guidance shifts from fragmented local versions to a governed unique facts model, Copilot/Agent experiences provide trusted role-relevant answers, and stakeholders consistently perform the new behaviors for creating, validating, consuming, and improving MCEM content.

[Sponsorship coalition alignment]

[Risks - Failed change outcomes]
