## Human Factors That Determine ROI

### Speed Of Adoption

The project should begin with high-value MCEM content domains for pilot conversion and SME validation. It should then expand through phased content-domain releases, with communication, training, feedback, and reinforcement attached to each release.

| Adoption stage | Target outcome |
|---|---|
| Pilot conversion | Validate the unique facts model with high-value content domains. |
| First content release | Establish trusted guidance and feedback paths for priority field roles. |
| Expanded releases | Extend governance, taxonomy, and Copilot/Agent readiness across additional content domains. |
| Sustainment | Retire duplicated local sources and reinforce the governed source of truth. |

### Ultimate Utilization

Ultimate utilization should be measured by:

- Percentage of field roles using Copilot/Agent or the governed source as their default access path for MCEM guidance
- Percentage of content owners and SMEs updating content through the unique facts model
- Reduction of local or duplicated MCEM sources
- Feedback volume, feedback closure rate, and answer trust score

### Proficiency

Proficiency means content owners and SMEs can correctly distinguish canon, exceptions, and guidance and apply metadata/tagging and validation workflows. SEL, BSO, and field roles should be able to find role-relevant guidance and submit feedback. Managers should be able to coach and reinforce the new behaviors.

### Notes

This project has stronger people-side adoption dependency than technical content transformation dependency. The most important ADKAR needs are Knowledge, Ability, and Reinforcement. If field trust is low, Copilot/Agent experiences will not become the actual source of truth.

## Costs And Risks

| Area | Costs | Risks |
|---|---|---|
| To individuals impacted by the change | Individuals must change habits around using local content. Content owners and SMEs must invest additional time in validation, tagging, and exception handling. Field roles must learn a new source and Copilot/Agent usage pattern. | Insufficient SME time, low field trust, unclear role expectations, change fatigue, and users bypassing the new governance model because the old way feels faster or safer. |
| To the project if the people side is not managed well | Scope ambiguity, SME capacity bottlenecks, delayed content validation, taxonomy inconsistency, duplicated rework, and release delays. | Technical or content milestones may be completed while adoption readiness lags behind, putting Copilot/Agent answer quality and field trust at risk. |
| To the organization if the people side is not managed well | Duplicated sources remain, governance decisions are delayed, segment guidance remains inconsistent, content maintenance costs increase, and field confusion continues. | Groups may continue maintaining local versions, weakening the single source of truth and the purpose of the agentic transformation. |
| To the organization if the change does not deliver expected results | The value of content cleanup, taxonomy work, and Copilot/Agent readiness is reduced while duplicated content and manual clarification costs continue. | Copilot/Agent experiences may be perceived as providing outdated or conflicting guidance, reducing field trust and AI adoption. |
